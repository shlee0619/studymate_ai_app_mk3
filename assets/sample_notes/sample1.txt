Spaced repetition is a learning technique that incorporates increasing intervals of time between subsequent review of previously learned material. The spacing effect was first noted by Hermann Ebbinghaus.

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel.

Newton's second law states that the acceleration of an object is dependent upon two variables: the net force acting upon the object and the mass of the object.

Photosynthesis is a process used by plants and other organisms to convert light energy into chemical energy.

In mathematics, the inequality a < b means that a is less than b. Conversely, a > b means a is greater than b.

Speed is distance over time, typically measured in m/s or km/h.

The mitochondrion is the powerhouse of the cell.

Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius at 1 atm of pressure.
